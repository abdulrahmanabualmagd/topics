Cascade:
Cascade refers to how CSS resolves conflicting styles from different sources. 
It follows the principles of specificity, origin, and order.

Specificity:
Specificity is a measure of how specific a selector is in targeting elements. 
It determines which styles take precedence in case of conflicts. 
Specificity is calculated based on selectors
hierarchy: inline styles > IDs > classes, attributes, and pseudo-classes > elements.
